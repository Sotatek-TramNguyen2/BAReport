# Intake

## Project Context

A neighborhood education group currently manages free Saturday workshop signups through email and manual spreadsheet updates. The process creates confusion for participants and overhead for coordinators. The group wants to refresh the signup flow before the next workshop cycle.

This is a solo, non-commercial internal improvement. One operator owns the full BA work. All deliverables are lightweight markdown.

## Problem Statement

Participants frequently experience confusion during the signup process and often miss schedule confirmations. Program coordinators lack a clear, repeatable flow for managing registrations, leading to missed attendees and last-minute coordination scrambles.

## Goals

- Reduce signup confusion for participants
- Make it easier for participants to confirm attendance
- Give coordinators a clearer, more predictable registration flow
- Have the improved process ready before the next workshop cycle

## Stakeholders

- **Program coordinators** — manage workshops, track signups, send confirmations
- **Participants** — sign up for Saturday workshops, need clear schedule info
- **One BA operator** — owns analysis and documentation (solo)

## Raw Requirements

- Replace or improve the current email + spreadsheet signup method
- Provide participants a clear way to see workshop schedule and confirm attendance
- Reduce the number of missed confirmations
- Keep the solution simple enough for coordinators to manage without technical support
- Maintain a record of who signed up and confirmed for each session

## Constraints

- Non-commercial internal use only
- One operator owns all BA work
- Lightweight markdown outputs only
- No budget for paid tools or custom development implied
- Solution must work within the group's existing capabilities

## Assumptions

- The neighborhood group has basic access to email and a shared document platform (e.g. Google Sheets)
- Participants have email access and basic digital literacy
- The workshop schedule is determined by coordinators in advance
- Volume is small enough for one person to manage (tens of participants, not hundreds)

## Open Questions

1. How many workshops run per cycle, and how many participants per session on average?
2. Is there a preferred communication channel besides email (e.g. WhatsApp group, community board)?
3. Are coordinators open to using a simple free tool (e.g. Google Forms) or must the solution stay within email + spreadsheet only?
4. What does "confirmation" mean specifically — confirming they will attend, or acknowledging they received the schedule?
5. Is there a waitlist or capacity limit per workshop session?
